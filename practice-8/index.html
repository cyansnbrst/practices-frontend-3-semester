About us

What do the lessons look like?

Classes at the school are based on the already classical communicative methodology, where new material (vocabulary, grammar)
is practiced in a playful or conversational form in small mini-groups or pairs. Based on the results of each course,
you can count on successfully passing a language exam of the appropriate level in all four skills - reading, writing,
speaking and listening.

We try to use all the new and cool applications that all our teachers found quite effective — Miro, GC, etc.

How many people are in the group?

On average, from 5 to 8 people.

School rules

Just as physics and chemistry have their own rules (like Na+Cl2->NaCl or E=mc2), they also exist in our school.

The most important rule - respect other people's borders.

Others are:
  1. Help each other
    • Studying at school involves working in a group or in pairs
    • Feel free to ask for help from students or teachers
    • Help your classmate if you have learned something faster than him
  2. Learn by yourself
    • A lot of language learning is built on independent work
    • Without independent study of the material at home, it is very difficult to achieve high results
    • It is best to use the portfolio system — and systematically put all the printouts in a special folder from the very first lesson
  3. Don't miss classes
    • The effectiveness of learning is directly related to the attendance of classes
    • If you missed more than 4 classes per semester, we reserve the right to ask you to repeat the level
